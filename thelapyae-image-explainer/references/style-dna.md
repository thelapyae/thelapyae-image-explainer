# Style DNA

- Format: 16:9 horizontal article illustration.
- Background: pure white with no paper texture, shadow, gradient, or scenery.
- Drawing: thin, slightly wobbly hand-drawn ink lines with a casual whiteboard
  or product-sketch feel. Lines should look manually drawn, not digitally perfect.
- Concept Mode space: keep at least 35% empty white space; main scene occupies
  about 40-60%.
- Tutorial Mode space: use 65-80% of the canvas with a clear information
  hierarchy. Preserve margins, but do not leave large areas empty.
- Color system: treat colors as sparse pen inks, not painted surfaces.
  - Black ink: outlines, objects, avatar hair/T-shirt/shoes, and primary text.
  - Orange ink: arrows, movement, sequence, and main flow.
  - Red ink: warnings, corrections, strong emphasis, and important headings.
  - Blue ink: secondary notes, explanations, decisions, and system feedback.
  - Light gray ink: avatar pants and rare structural guides.
- Keep most object interiors white. Do not fill cards, boxes, machines, folders,
  backgrounds, banners, or large shapes with solid blue, green, orange, or brown.
- The avatar may use a light natural skin tint and gray pants, but the overall
  image must still read as an ink sketch rather than a colored cartoon.
- Concept Mode text: use at most five short handwritten labels.
- Tutorial Mode text: use 6-12 concise elements arranged as:
  step badge, action heading, one explanation sentence, command/path box,
  process callouts, and result note.
- Meaning: communicate one idea through one physical action or visual metaphor.

Avoid generic presentation slides, commercial vector art, realistic UI, dense
architecture diagrams, children's illustration, decorative backgrounds, colored
panels, glossy buttons, gradients, and large flat color blocks.
Tutorial Mode may use a prominent action heading and structured callout boxes,
but boxes must stay white with colored ink outlines. It must feel hand-drawn and
character-led rather than corporate.
