# Style DNA

- Format: 16:9 horizontal article illustration.
- Background: pure white with no paper texture, shadow, gradient, or scenery.
- Drawing: thin, slightly wobbly black ink lines with a casual product-sketch feel.
- Concept Mode space: keep at least 35% empty white space; main scene occupies
  about 40-60%.
- Tutorial Mode space: use 65-80% of the canvas with a clear information
  hierarchy. Preserve margins, but do not leave large areas empty.
- Color: preserve the avatar's natural skin tone, black T-shirt, medium-gray
  pants, and black shoes. Use orange for movement, red for warnings or important
  results, and blue for secondary system notes.
- Concept Mode text: use at most five short handwritten labels.
- Tutorial Mode text: use 6-12 concise elements arranged as:
  step badge, action heading, one explanation sentence, command/path box,
  process callouts, and result note.
- Meaning: communicate one idea through one physical action or visual metaphor.

Avoid generic presentation slides, commercial vector art, realistic UI, dense
architecture diagrams, children's illustration, and decorative backgrounds.
Tutorial Mode may use a prominent action heading and structured callout boxes,
but it must still feel hand-drawn and character-led rather than corporate.
