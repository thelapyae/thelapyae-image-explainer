# Ink Style Calibration

Use a white-paper hand-drawn explanation style.

## Target Look

- Pure white background with no paper texture.
- Black pen outlines dominate the image.
- Lines are thin, slightly uneven, and visibly hand drawn.
- Most objects are unfilled white shapes outlined in black.
- Use orange pen arrows to show the main flow.
- Use red pen for one important warning, correction, or emphasis.
- Use blue pen for one or two secondary notes or decisions.
- Use handwritten annotations close to the object they explain.
- Keep the composition clean, but include enough labeled inputs, actions, and
  outputs for the image to explain itself.

## Color Budget

Aim for approximately:

- 75-85% white background and white object interiors
- 10-18% black ink and black avatar clothing
- 2-5% orange ink
- 1-3% red ink
- 1-3% blue ink
- minimal natural skin tint and light-gray pants

These are visual targets, not pixel measurements.

## Avoid

- No solid-color panels or banners.
- No blue, green, orange, or brown filled cards.
- No colored machine bodies, folder fills, or UI surfaces.
- No gradients, glossy buttons, drop shadows, or polished vector rendering.
- No corporate infographic palette.
- Do not copy the calibration image's character, objects, labels, or exact layout.
  Reuse only its ink density, color restraint, whitespace, and annotation feel.
